01 — AboutBiography

The Mind
Behind the Work

I am a Neuroscience PhD researcher at the University of Houston, working at the intersection of brain electrophysiology, psychophysiology, and music cognition. My research investigates how the brain encodes, processes, and responds to complex auditory and artistic stimuli.

As President of the BRAIN-AccelNet Student Network, I lead an international community of emerging neuroscientists committed to cross-disciplinary collaboration and open science. I believe that understanding the brain requires combining rigorous methodology with creative thinking.

My technical work spans EEG signal processing, ICA decomposition, dipole source localization, accelerometer integration, and multimodal data analysis — always in pursuit of a deeper understanding of how we experience the world.

02 — ResearchActive Projects

Current
Projects

EEG · Music Cognition
Diabelli
A multimodal study combining EEG recordings with structured musical variation ratings. Examining neural responses to Diabelli's 33 variations across participants, with psychophysiological correlates of aesthetic perception.
Signal Processing
ICA Dipole Pipeline
An MNE-Python pipeline for automated ICA component classification, dipole fitting to the fsaverage template, and AAL atlas-based anatomical labeling — with per-component MRI, topography, and spectrogram figures.
Psychophysiology
ACC + EEG Integration
Extending classical EEG raster visualization to include conductor motion data from accelerometers. Traces from head and hand provide a synchronized view of neural and motor dynamics during musical performance.
Network
BRAIN-AccelNet
As Student Network President, I coordinate international workshops, speaker series (including invited lectures from Prof. Stephen Grossberg), and cross-institutional research collaborations across neuroscience disciplines.
Open Science
Data Descriptor
Preparing a data descriptor publication for the Diabelli EEG dataset — detailing acquisition protocols, preprocessing pipelines, and multimodal figure generation, enabling reproducibility and community reuse.
Methods
Source Localization
Applying dipole fitting and AAL parcellation to characterize the spatial origins of ICA components — linking scalp-level electrophysiology to anatomically meaningful brain regions via MNI152 templates.
